# Snubbins — Brand Brief
*One-page brief · Workshop example (Block 1 output)*

**One-sentence concept:** Single-ingredient dog treats made from perfectly good "ugly" produce that grocery stores reject, rescuing food from the landfill and putting it in your dog's bowl.

**Target customer:** Millennial and Gen-Z dog parents (28-45) who already buy organic, read ingredient labels, and feel guilty about household waste. They treat their dog like family and want premium snacks without the premium-priced greenwashing.

**Problem we solve:** "Healthy" dog treats are either mystery-meat junk or guilt-priced boutique products, while 6 billion pounds of edible produce gets dumped every year for being the wrong shape. We turn that waste into clean, traceable treats you can actually feel good about buying.

## Product line
- **Sweet Potato Snubbins** — twisted, dehydrated chews from misshapen sweet potatoes (the bestselling hero SKU)
- **Pumpkin & Apple Bites** — soft-baked, gut-friendly biscuits from cosmetically rejected fall produce
- **Carrot Crunch Sticks** — crunchy single-ingredient sticks for teeth and chewers
- **The Wonky Box** — a rotating seasonal subscription of whatever ugly harvest we rescued that month
- All recipes: one to three ingredients, human-grade, made in small batches in the US

## Brand voice
- **Cheeky** — we make fun of "perfect" produce, not preachy about saving the planet
- **Honest** — we name the exact farm and the exact reason each batch was rejected
- **Warm** — we talk to dog people like fellow dog people, not eco-lecturers
- **Plainspoken** — short words, real ingredients, zero wellness jargon

## 3 launch-message ideas
1. **"Ugly on the outside. Perfect in the bowl."** — the wonky-produce hero line for ads and packaging.
2. **"Your dog can't read a label. So we made one worth reading."** — single-ingredient transparency play.
3. **"We rescued 2 tons of 'too ugly to sell' produce this month. Your dog ate the evidence."** — impact-as-receipt message for social and email.
