# Offcut — Brand Brief
*One-page brief · Workshop example (Block 1 output)*

**One-sentence concept:** Offcut makes the everyday basics you actually reach for — tees, socks, totes — from premium deadstock fabric rescued from fashion houses before it hits the landfill.

**Target customer:** Design-literate millennials (28–40) who already buy "better basics," feel guilty about fast fashion, and want their staples to be quietly well-made and low-impact — without paying luxury prices or reading a sustainability report to feel good about it.

**Problem we solve:** "Sustainable" basics are usually either expensive, scratchy, or preachy — and most are still made from brand-new virgin cotton. Meanwhile, perfectly good designer-grade fabric gets thrown away by the ton. We turn that waste into the most comfortable basics in your drawer.

## Product line
- **The Core Tee** — heavyweight and lightweight crews in rescued cotton and modal blends
- **Everyday Socks** — ribbed crew and ankle, sold in mismatched-by-design 3-packs
- **The Carry Tote** — sturdy canvas and webbing offcuts, built to outlast a dozen plastic bags
- **Limited "Last Run" drops** — small-batch colorways made from a single deadstock lot, never restocked
- **Seasonal layering piece** — a long-sleeve or henley added once supply is proven

## Brand voice
- **Plainspoken** — talks like a person, not a sustainability report
- **Quietly confident** — lets the fabric and fit do the bragging
- **Wry, not preachy** — honest about waste without guilt-tripping the customer
- **Specific** — names the mill, the fabric, the lot; proof over promises

## 3 launch-message ideas
1. **"Made from what fashion threw away. Made better than what you're wearing."**
2. **"Every Offcut is a one-time run. When the fabric's gone, it's gone — that's the point."**
3. **"Great basics shouldn't cost the earth a thing. Ours literally don't — they're already made."**
