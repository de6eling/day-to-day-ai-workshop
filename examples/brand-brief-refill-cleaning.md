# Stoik — Brand Brief
*One-page brief · Workshop example (Block 1 output)*

**One-sentence concept:** Stoik turns home cleaning into a buy-it-once habit — heavy glass bottles you keep for years, refilled by concentrated pods you just drop in water.

**Target customer:** Design-conscious renters and homeowners, 28–45, who already buy "better-for-you" household goods, hate the clutter of plastic spray bottles under the sink, and feel low-grade guilt every time they toss one.

**Problem we solve:** Cleaning products are sold as disposable plastic you rebuy, restock, and throw away — wasteful, ugly on the counter, and shipping mostly water. Stoik separates the bottle (own it forever) from the cleaner (cheap, light, refilled by mail).

## Product line
- **The Keepers:** weighted glass + silicone bottles for all-purpose, glass, and bathroom — designed to live on the counter, not hide under the sink.
- **Refill pods:** concentrated drop-in pods (3-pack) per cleaner; add tap water, shake, done.
- **Starter kit:** 3 bottles + 3 pods, the one-time purchase that replaces years of plastic.
- **Refill subscription:** pods mailed in a flat, letterbox-friendly carton on your schedule — pause or skip anytime.
- **Scent line:** a small, considered set (Unscented, Eucalyptus, Sea Salt) — no seasonal gimmicks.

## Brand voice
- **Plain-spoken** — clear instructions, no eco-jargon or guilt-tripping.
- **Quietly confident** — we state the obvious math (you're paying to ship water) and let it land.
- **Warm, not preachy** — sustainability is the result, not the sermon.
- **Design-led** — we talk about the object on your counter as much as the chemistry inside it.

## 3 launch-message ideas
1. **"Buy the bottle once."** The last cleaning bottles you'll ever need — refilled by mail, never thrown away.
2. **"Stop shipping water."** A spray bottle is 90% water and 100% plastic. We ship the other 10% and let your tap do the rest.
3. **"Cleaning supplies that earn a spot on the counter."** Heavy glass, refill pods, zero under-sink clutter.
